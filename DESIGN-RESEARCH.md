# Design research: Human Resources Outsourced

## Search brief

- Query: `HR outsourcing services small business`
- Buyer: owners and lean HR teams that need help with recruiting admin, onboarding paperwork, employee questions, and weekly HR checks
- Offer constraint: consultation-led staffing plan with no public pricing
- Review date: 2026-07-20

## Five direct competitors reviewed

| Competitor | Visual notes | Score |
| --- | --- | ---: |
| TriNet: https://www.trinet.com/ | Bold navy hero, warm orange actions, clear split between full PEO help and lighter HR support, real customer photography, strong proof and a complete footer. Long, but the hierarchy stays clear. | 8.3/10 |
| Bambee: https://www.bambee.com/ | Purple and teal SaaS look, human HR manager portraits, embedded lead question, practical feature sequence, strong legal footer. Busy above the fold and public price messages compete with the service story. | 8.2/10 |
| CoAd: https://www.coad.com/ | Full-bleed workplace photo, sage and cream palette, editorial type, two clear actions. Premium and warm, though proof is light in the first view. | 7.5/10 |
| Paychex: https://www.paychex.com/human-resources | Corporate navy and blue, rounded editorial images, broad service coverage, strong disclosures and footer. The hero felt unbalanced in the live capture and the page offered too many competing actions. | 6.5/10 |
| ADP: https://www.adp.com/ | Crisp red and navy brand system with a short solution finder and visible sales path. The live capture had large unloaded areas, limited visual context, and little service detail in view. | 5.0/10 |

## Selected direction

**Winner: TriNet.** It had the best mix of authority, warm contrast, easy service choices, real workplace imagery, proof, and legal depth.

Patterns worth adapting:

- a dark, high-contrast opening rather than a pale generic staffing hero
- one short promise, one action, and a strong people image
- warm orange reserved for conversion actions
- service choices written around buyer problems, not vague feature names
- clear boundaries around what support staff can prepare and what an HR owner must decide
- customer-proof rhythm replaced here with factual task and control examples because this site has no verified testimonials to publish

## Originality notes

The homepage is not a copy of TriNet. It uses a distinct aubergine, coral, paper, and mint palette; an offset photo frame; a four-lane HR desk strip; numbered task cards; and a visible decision-boundary panel. Copy, layout, icon, photography, and page modules are original to Human Resources Outsourced. No competitor assets, testimonials, statistics, or proprietary illustrations are reused.

## Mobile plan

- Collapse the header navigation and keep one contact action.
- Stack the hero copy above the image.
- Remove decorative photo offsets that could cover the subject.
- Keep task cards and the launch steps in one column.
- Preserve 44px touch targets and readable type.
